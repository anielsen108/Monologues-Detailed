# Ivan Karamazov’s Rebellion Monologue — The Brothers Karamazov

## Complete Text

> I have taken cases from the papers, Alyosha—<br>
> Children beaten, torn, shot, all in the name of order.<br>
> Can you forgive them for the tear of one tortured child?<br>
> It’s not that I don’t accept God, you see, only I most respectfully return Him the ticket.<br>
> I hasten to give back my admission; it is not worth the price of a child’s tear.<br>

Ivan argues that a world built on innocent suffering cannot be accepted, even if God exists.
